## What each article covers

**Article 1 — AI tools for beginners**
A practical introduction to AI tools for people who are new to the space. Covers what AI tools actually do, which categories are most useful for everyday tasks, and how to start using them without feeling overwhelmed. Written for a general audience with no technical background required.

**Article 2 — How to evaluate AI-generated content**
A guide to assessing AI outputs critically. Covers the key dimensions of a good AI response — accuracy, tone, structure, and factual reliability — and explains how to spot weak or misleading content. Useful for writers, editors, and anyone working with AI platforms.

**Article 3 — Prompt engineering for content writers**
A practical breakdown of how content writers can use prompt engineering to get better results from AI tools. Covers prompt structure, common mistakes, and techniques for producing consistent, on-brand output. Written for writers who want more control over their AI tools.

**Article 4 — AI in content strategy — what actually works**
An honest look at where AI adds real value in content strategy and where it falls short. Covers use cases like ideation, calendar planning, and brief writing, with practical advice on integrating AI without losing editorial quality.
